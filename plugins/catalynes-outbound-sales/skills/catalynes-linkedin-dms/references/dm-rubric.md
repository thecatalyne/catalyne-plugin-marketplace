# Catalyne DM Quality Rubric — Full Reference

10 criteria, scored 1–5 each, max 50 per DM. Used as a self-check by the writing sub-agent and as a downstream QA check by the orchestrator.

All criteria are calibrated to 2026 LinkedIn outreach benchmarks.

## The 10 criteria

| # | Criterion | What 5/5 looks like |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Trigger strength** | Names a specific business event (raise, hire, leadership change, recent post). Generic title-only reference = 1. |
| 2 | **Trigger urgency / recency** | This week=5, this month=4, this quarter=3, this year=2, older=1. |
| 3 | **Length** | v2: 60–80 words=5, 81–100=4, 101–150=3, >150=1. v3: 200–280 chars=5, 281–300=4, <200=4, >300=1 (fails the hard rule). |
| 4 | **Personalization specificity** | Quotes or paraphrases a specific post or business detail. Generic title+company = 1. |
| 5 | **Peer-to-peer tone** | One professional talking to another, no jargon, no pitch language. Salesy/formal = 1–2. |
| 6 | **Rapport bridge (earned)** | Opens with a genuine observation linked to their work, not flattery. Forced compliment = 1. |
| 7 | **Social-selling integration** | Delivers a peer insight or pattern observation, not a sales pitch. "We help X do Y" = 1. |
| 8 | **CTA friction** | Open question answerable in 8 sec; no meeting ask. Meeting/calendar/pitch ask = 1. |
| 9 | **WIIFM clarity** | Recipient can answer "why reply?" in <3 sec. No reciprocity offer = 1. |
| 10 | **No red flags** | No spam triggers, no humble-brag, no over-claim, no snark, no "quick question" cliché. |

## Pass/fail tiers (calibrated to 2026 LinkedIn benchmarks)

| Score | Verdict | Predicted reply rate |
|---|---|---|
| 45–50 | **Send** | ≥25% (top tier) |
| 38–44 | **Polish then send** | 15–25% |
| 30–37 | **Rework** | Below benchmark |
| <30 | **Don't send** | Likely ghost |

## Why these 10 (not 15, not 5)

The 2026 sources converge on a small set of variables that move reply rates. Stripped to essence: **trigger × specificity × tone × ask**. The 10 criteria split those four into measurable dimensions plus the operational checks (length, red flags) that benchmarks flag as biggest deal-breakers.

## Common failure modes the rubric catches

- **High trigger, weak WIIFM** → 4/5 on criterion 1, 3/5 on criterion 9. Fix: add a 5–10 word reciprocity offer.
- **Bolted-on rapport** → 3/5 on criterion 6 (rapport feels overlaid, not earned). Fix: integrate rapport into the brief BEFORE writing, not as a header tacked on.
- **Snark in first DM** → 3/5 on criterion 5 and 4/5 on criterion 10. Fix: state the same observation neutrally.
- **v3 over 300 chars** → 1/5 on criterion 3 (fails the hard rule, regardless of other scores). Fix: tighten WIIFM specificity, switch question to binary, drop softening framing.

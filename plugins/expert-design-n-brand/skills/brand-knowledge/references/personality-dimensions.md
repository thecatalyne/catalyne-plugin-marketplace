# Brand Personality Dimensions

Based on Jennifer Aaker's Brand Personality Framework (1997) — the most widely validated model for measuring how people perceive brand character. The five dimensions map to human personality traits, giving brands a measurable personality profile.

## How to Use This Reference

- **During discovery**: Use the Aaker Spectrum Sliders technique — have the person rate the brand 1-10 on each dimension.
- **In synthesis**: Compare team members' scores. Large gaps (3+ points) on any dimension signal a divergence worth discussing.
- **In design**: Personality scores directly inform visual and voice decisions (see mapping tables below).

---

## The Five Dimensions

### 1. Sincerity

**Core traits**: Honest, wholesome, cheerful, down-to-earth
**Spectrum**: `1 [Corporate/Distant] ←——→ [Warm/Genuine] 10`

**Facets**:
- **Down-to-earth**: Family-oriented, small-town, conventional, blue-collar
- **Honest**: Sincere, real, ethical, thoughtful, caring
- **Wholesome**: Original, genuine, ageless, classic, old-fashioned
- **Cheerful**: Sentimental, friendly, warm, happy

**Scoring guide**:
| Score | Reads As | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1-3 | Deliberately impersonal, transactional | Bloomberg Terminal, B2B enterprise |
| 4-5 | Professional but approachable | Salesforce, LinkedIn |
| 6-7 | Genuine warmth, trustworthy | Patagonia, Airbnb |
| 8-10 | Deeply personal, community-centered | Etsy, Warby Parker, TOMS |

**Visual implications**:
- High sincerity → warm tones, organic shapes, handwritten/humanist type, real photography
- Low sincerity → cool tones, geometric precision, structured layouts, abstract imagery

**Voice implications**:
- High sincerity → first person, conversational, admits mistakes, uses "we" and "you"
- Low sincerity → formal, institutional, focuses on capabilities not feelings

---

### 2. Excitement

**Core traits**: Daring, spirited, imaginative, up-to-date
**Spectrum**: `1 [Measured/Calm] ←——→ [Bold/Energetic] 10`

**Facets**:
- **Daring**: Trendy, exciting, off-beat, flashy, provocative
- **Spirited**: Cool, young, lively, outgoing, adventurous
- **Imaginative**: Unique, humorous, surprising, artistic, fun
- **Up-to-date**: Independent, contemporary, innovative, aggressive

**Scoring guide**:
| Score | Reads As | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1-3 | Deliberate, calm, timeless | Berkshire Hathaway, The Economist |
| 4-5 | Modern but measured | Notion, Stripe |
| 6-7 | Energetic, forward-looking | Spotify, Figma |
| 8-10 | Bold, disruptive, high-energy | Red Bull, Liquid Death, Nike |

**Visual implications**:
- High excitement → saturated colors, dynamic layouts, bold type, motion/animation, asymmetry
- Low excitement → muted palettes, static layouts, classic type, symmetry, generous white space

**Voice implications**:
- High excitement → short punchy sentences, exclamations, slang, active voice, imperative
- Low excitement → longer sentences, measured tone, passive acceptable, nuance over punch

---

### 3. Competence

**Core traits**: Reliable, intelligent, successful, leader
**Spectrum**: `1 [Scrappy/Experimental] ←——→ [Polished/Expert] 10`

**Facets**:
- **Reliable**: Hardworking, secure, efficient, trustworthy, careful
- **Intelligent**: Technical, corporate, serious, analytical
- **Successful**: Leader, confident, influential, accomplished

**Scoring guide**:
| Score | Reads As | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1-3 | Scrappy, experimental, "we're figuring it out" | Early-stage startups, indie brands |
| 4-5 | Capable but not trying to impress | Basecamp, Craigslist |
| 6-7 | Professional, credible, established | IBM, Deloitte |
| 8-10 | Authoritative, premium, institutional | McKinsey, Goldman Sachs, Mayo Clinic |

**Visual implications**:
- High competence → structured grids, clean hierarchy, system fonts, data visualization, precision
- Low competence → looser layouts, hand-drawn elements, informal type, experimental composition

**Voice implications**:
- High competence → data-backed claims, precise language, industry terminology, case studies
- Low competence → colloquial language, anecdotes, "we think" over "research shows"

---

### 4. Sophistication

**Core traits**: Upper class, charming, glamorous, refined
**Spectrum**: `1 [Accessible/Casual] ←——→ [Premium/Refined] 10`

**Facets**:
- **Upper class**: Glamorous, good-looking, pretentious, sophisticated
- **Charming**: Feminine, smooth, sexy, gentle

**Scoring guide**:
| Score | Reads As | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1-3 | Deliberately casual, anti-luxury | Dollar Shave Club, Costco |
| 4-5 | Quality without pretension | Muji, Uniqlo |
| 6-7 | Refined, tasteful, elevated | Aesop, Apple |
| 8-10 | Luxurious, exclusive, aspirational | Chanel, Rolex, Hermès |

**Visual implications**:
- High sophistication → restrained palettes, serif type, generous negative space, premium materials, editorial photography
- Low sophistication → bright colors, sans-serif, dense layouts, user-generated content, illustration

**Voice implications**:
- High sophistication → elevated vocabulary, understated claims, implication over statement, editorial quality
- Low sophistication → plain language, direct claims, humor, self-deprecation welcome

---

### 5. Ruggedness

**Core traits**: Outdoorsy, tough, strong, masculine
**Spectrum**: `1 [Gentle/Refined] ←——→ [Tough/Outdoorsy] 10`

**Facets**:
- **Outdoorsy**: Masculine, Western, active, athletic
- **Tough**: Rugged, strong, no-nonsense, durable

**Scoring guide**:
| Score | Reads As | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 1-3 | Soft, gentle, cerebral | Calm app, Headspace, Aesop |
| 4-5 | Balanced — neither tough nor delicate | Slack, Notion |
| 6-7 | Sturdy, reliable, built to last | Carhartt, Yeti |
| 8-10 | Tough, outdoors, physically grounded | Patagonia, Jeep, Harley-Davidson |

**Visual implications**:
- High ruggedness → earth tones, textured surfaces, heavy type weights, raw materials, outdoor photography
- Low ruggedness → pastels/light palettes, smooth gradients, light type weights, digital/abstract imagery

**Voice implications**:
- High ruggedness → short sentences, action verbs, concrete language, no hedging
- Low ruggedness → flowing sentences, abstract concepts, nuance, qualifiers acceptable

## Using Dimension Scores Together

### Profile Shapes

A brand's personality profile is the shape created by all five scores together. Common profile patterns:

**The Challenger**: High excitement (8+), high competence (7+), low sincerity (3-4)
- Brands that lead through innovation and boldness
- Example: Tesla, SpaceX

**The Trusted Guide**: High sincerity (8+), high competence (7+), moderate everything else
- Brands that lead through expertise delivered warmly
- Example: Mayo Clinic, Patagonia

**The Luxury Craft**: High sophistication (8+), high competence (7+), low ruggedness (2-3)
- Brands that lead through refined excellence
- Example: Hermès, Bang & Olufsen

**The Everyday Hero**: High sincerity (7+), high ruggedness (6+), moderate excitement (5-6)
- Brands that lead through reliable toughness with warmth
- Example: Carhartt, Subaru

**The Playful Innovator**: High excitement (8+), moderate sincerity (5-6), low sophistication (3-4)
- Brands that lead through fun and creativity
- Example: Mailchimp, Figma

### Divergence Analysis

When comparing team members' scores in synthesis:

| Gap Size | Interpretation |
|----------|---------------|
| 0-1 points | Strong agreement — no action needed |
| 2 points | Minor difference — note but don't worry |
| 3-4 points | Meaningful divergence — discuss and resolve |
| 5+ points | Fundamental disagreement — requires facilitated conversation |

**Most common divergences**:
- Sincerity vs. competence tension (warm vs. authoritative)
- Excitement level (bold vs. measured)
- Sophistication (accessible vs. premium)

## Mapping Dimensions to Design Decisions

| Decision | Primary Dimension | Secondary |
|----------|------------------|-----------|
| Color temperature (warm vs. cool) | Sincerity | Ruggedness |
| Color saturation | Excitement | Sophistication (inverse) |
| Type formality (serif vs. sans) | Sophistication | Competence |
| Type weight (light vs. heavy) | Ruggedness | Excitement |
| Layout density | Competence | Sophistication |
| White space | Sophistication | Sincerity |
| Motion/animation | Excitement | — |
| Photography style | All five inform this together |
| Illustration style | Sincerity + Excitement |
| Border radius (sharp vs. rounded) | Ruggedness (inverse) | Sincerity |
